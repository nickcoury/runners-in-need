# Market Research: Runners In Need

Research conducted 2026-03-11. Covers competitive landscape, demand signals, and go-to-market channels.

---

## Does This Already Exist?

**Short answer:** Nothing does exactly what RIN does. There are bulk shoe donation charities and one emerging sports gear matching platform, but no two-sided marketplace where organizers post specific running gear needs (sizes, quantities, condition) and individual donors browse and pledge against them.

### Closest Competitors

**Kitmatch (UK)** — kitround.com
- Free sports gear donation platform connecting donors with schools and grassroots clubs
- Organizations submit wish lists; Kitmatch matches donations to requests
- Uses InPost lockers for logistics
- **Gap:** UK-only, not running-specific, no donor-browsing interface

**Running Warehouse QuickStart Program** — runningwarehouse.com/shoedonation.html
- Provides lightly worn running shoes to schools/clubs encouraging youth running
- Organizations apply with specific quantities (5-25 shoes) and can indicate sizes
- **Gap:** Application-based fulfillment, not a marketplace. Organizations apply and wait — donors don't browse needs.

**Give Back Track** — givebacktrack.org
- Collects new and gently-used track & field footwear for high school athletes
- Accepts specific shoe types (distance spikes, sprint spikes, racing flats)
- Provides shipping labels and tax receipts
- **Gap:** One-directional collection, not matching. No browse-and-pledge model.

**Good Sports** — goodsports.org
- Equitable access to youth sports equipment in high-need communities
- Donors can choose sports categories to pledge
- **Gap:** General sports (not running-specific), organization-focused distribution, no specific-needs browsing.

### Bulk Donation Organizations (Not Matching)

| Organization | Model | Gap vs RIN |
|---|---|---|
| **Soles4Souls** | Bulk shoe collection, global distribution | No specific matching, no organizer needs |
| **One World Running** | Collects shoes for developing countries + domestic shelters | Bulk collection, no needs-based matching |
| **Share Your Soles** | Shoes for homeless/low-income globally | General collection, not sports-specific |
| **Shoe4Africa** | Originally shoes for Kenyan runners, now focused on healthcare | Evolved away from shoe matching |
| **Nike Reuse-A-Shoe** | Retail drop-off, shoes ground into playground surfaces | Recycling, not reuse for runners |

### The Gap RIN Fills

No platform currently offers:
1. Organizers posting **specific** gear requests (exact sizes, types, quantities, condition)
2. Individual donors **browsing** those specific requests
3. Donors **pledging** directly against needs with coordination
4. A two-sided **marketplace** interface (not just application/fulfillment)
5. Running-specific focus with community understanding

## Is There Demand?

### Evidence: Organizations Need Gear

**DonorsChoose has active running shoe requests:**
- Mr. Rawls (Baton Rouge) — track shoes for 25 students
- Mrs. Lancaster (Onamia, MN) — proper running footwear for 10 students
- Mr. Gentry (Bronx) — shoes for 350 PE students, some with no shoes at all
- Ms. Winker (Mauston, WI) — appropriate footwear for students without proper shoes

These exist because traditional donation channels aren't meeting needs. Programs resort to general fundraising platforms.

**Cost is a documented barrier to youth running:**
- Children from households under $25K participate in sports at **half the rate** of those over $100K
- Youth sports costs increased **46% since 2019**
- Quality running shoes: $120-$275 — significant entry barrier
- Only **22%** of low-income families' children participate in organized sports vs 43% higher-income

**Established nonprofits prove sustained demand:**
- One World Running (since 1986) — 50+ countries
- Soles4Souls — demand exceeds supply, must prioritize
- Shoes That Fit — measures children individually for proper athletic footwear
- NYRR — distributed 47,000+ pairs since 2017

### Evidence: Runners Want to Donate

**Running forums regularly discuss gear donation:**
- LetsRun threads about "what to do with worn out shoes," donating to XC teams and inner-city programs
- Reddit r/running, r/RunningShoeGeeks — active discussions about accumulating shoes
- Slowtwitch forums discuss TriThrift (endurance gear matching for aspiring athletes)

**Runners accumulate gear naturally:**
- Shoes replaced every 300-500 miles
- Race medals, apparel from events
- Wrong-fit purchases, last-season models
- Running store employees with access to discontinued inventory (see mock pledge data — this is real)

## Launch Strategy

### Recommended: Geographic Concentration

Based on how Girls on the Run and Back on My Feet scaled, concentrate on **one running-heavy city first**, achieve saturation, then expand.

**Best candidate cities** (strong running culture + youth programs):
- **Portland, OR** — Nick's home, local connections, strong running culture, Nike HQ proximity
- Austin, TX — large running community, year-round weather
- Denver/Boulder, CO — running mecca, many clubs and programs
- San Francisco Bay Area — tech-friendly, active running scene

**Per-city playbook:**
1. Partner with 8-10 local running stores
2. Build relationships with 20-30 running clubs
3. Secure 3-5 race partnerships
4. Onboard 20-50 organizations with real needs
5. Let supply pull in demand (donors want to help when they see real needs)

**Timeline:** 6-12 months to full market penetration per city.

### Cold Start: Seed Supply First

Research consensus: for donation platforms, seed the supply side (organizations) first.
- Organizations have stronger incentive (free resources)
- 20-50 quality needs make the platform useful for donors
- Unlike commercial marketplaces, no transaction friction

**DonorsChoose lesson:** They started with just 11 teacher projects. Quality beats quantity at launch.

### Content/SEO (Long-Term)

Target keywords with moderate search volume and low competition:
- "donate running shoes near me"
- "running shoes for youth programs"
- "where to donate athletic shoes"
- "shoe donation programs for schools"

Get RIN listed in existing "where to donate" guides (Sneakers4Good, Soles4Souls resource pages).

## Key Takeaways

1. **The gap is real.** No platform does what RIN proposes. Bulk charities exist, but specific-needs matching for running gear doesn't.
2. **Demand is documented.** Organizations post on DonorsChoose because nothing better exists. Runners actively discuss donating gear.
3. **Cost barrier is quantified.** Youth sports costs up 46%, running shoes $120-275, low-income participation at half the rate.
4. **The running community is organized.** USATF (3K clubs), RRCA (1.5K clubs), Girls on the Run (175 councils) — structured networks ready to reach.
5. **Start local, go deep.** Portland is the natural beachhead — Nick's home, strong running culture, manageable market size.
6. **Seed supply first.** Get 20-50 organizations posting real needs. Donors will come when they see specific, actionable asks.
7. **Race infrastructure is the trojan horse.** Finish lines, expos, and running stores are perfect physical touchpoints.
